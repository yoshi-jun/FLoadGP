It served historically as an Independent Order of Odd Fellows meeting hall, as a multiple dwelling, as a specialty store, and as a business. The building was destroyed by fire on the morning of October 14, 2013.[2]: 5  It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1992 and was removed from the National Register in 2014.[1][3]  References[edit]   ^ a b "National Register Information System".
