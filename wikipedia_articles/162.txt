It was built in 1905, and is a 1+1⁄2-story, Queen Anne style weatherboard residence set on a brick foundation.  It has a two-story turret under a tent roof and a bay surmounted by a pedimented cross gable.  The house was built by Broadus Edwards, prominent Batesburg merchant, mortician, and town councilman.[2][3] It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.[1]  References[edit]   ^ a b "National Register Information System".
